How to Add a Physical Address
to Your Google Business Profile

For service businesses adding a location pin to an existing verified profile

Why a Physical Address Matters for Rankings

Google uses three factors to decide which businesses show up in the local pack: relevance, distance, and prominence. Distance is measured from your registered address to the searcher.

Without a physical address pin, Google has no fixed point to anchor your location — it guesses based on your service area settings, which is imprecise. Adding a real address gives Google an exact anchor, making it far more likely to surface your business for searches in that city or neighborhood.

💡
What to expect: Adding or changing an address on an existing GBP almost always triggers re-verification. Google needs to confirm the new location is real. The most common method in 2026 is a short video recording.
Which Address Should You Use?

You have three options. The right one depends on your situation.

Option 1: Home Address (Recommended for Most Service Businesses)

Use your home address and select NO when asked if customers visit. The address stays completely hidden from the public — customers never see it — but Google uses it as your ranking anchor. Zero suspension risk. This is the safest and most practical option for a home-based pressure washing business.

✅
What you need to verify it: A utility bill or any official document showing your name at that address. Your business license works too if it lists your home address.
Option 2: Commercial Address (If You Have One)

If you lease or own a commercial space, use that address and select YES to show a public map pin. This is the strongest ranking signal Google can work with. Verify with a lease agreement or utility bill matching the address.

Option 3: Co-Working Space — High Risk, Proceed with Caution

Co-working spaces are not explicitly banned by Google, but they carry a significantly higher suspension risk than home or commercial addresses. Google allows them only when all of the following are true simultaneously:

  • You have a dedicated private office — not a flex desk, hot desk, or shared membership
  • Your business name appears on the door or building directory permanently — even when you're not there
  • You or a staff member are physically present during your stated business hours
  • You can receive customers at that location during business hours
  • You have a unique suite number to receive mail
Reality check: Co-working spaces are cited in 42% of GBP suspensions. A documented 2024 case shows a business suspended immediately upon updating to a co-working address — and suspended again on every re-submission attempt. Unless you have a dedicated private office with permanent signage and guaranteed staff presence, a home address is the safer path.
Step 1: Add the Address to Your Profile
  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in.
  2. Select your business profile and click Edit profile.
  3. Go to Business location and click the pencil icon.
  4. Enter your physical address. Make sure it exactly matches your business license and any utility bills.
  5. When asked "Do you want to add a location customers can visit?", select YES to show a map pin, or NO to keep the address private but still use it as your ranking anchor.
  6. Save the change. Google will likely prompt you to verify the new address immediately.
Note: If you want a map pin visible on Google Maps (recommended for ranking), select YES. If you work from home and don't want your home address public, select NO — the address still anchors your rankings but stays hidden from customers.
Step 2: Prepare Before You Record

Google will assign a verification method automatically — in most cases it will be a short video. Have everything ready before you hit record.

📋
Do you need documents before adding the address? No — you can add the address first, which triggers verification, then gather your documents during the process. Having them ready before you record reduces the risk of rejection, but they are not required upfront. The checklist below is what to have on hand when you record, not before you click save.
Pre-Flight Checklist (for when you record)
  • GBP address matches your business license or utility bill exactly
  • Business documents within reach: license, registration, invoice, or utility bill
  • Business card placed on your desk or work surface
  • Work vehicle visible outside (branded wrap or decal is a strong plus)
  • Pressure washing equipment visible and ready to show
  • Key pre-inserted in your front door lock to save time
  • Computer or phone open showing your website or scheduling software
Step 3: Record the Verification Video

One continuous, uninterrupted shot — no pausing, no cuts, no talking, no faces. Keep it under 2 minutes. Aim for 45–90 seconds. Practice the route once before recording.

SAB Video Sequence — In Order
  • Step 1 — Start outside your home or base. Show your house number and the neighboring houses clearly so Google can confirm the address.
  • Step 2 — Show your work vehicle and pressure washing equipment. If you have a logo on the truck, a decal, or a branded shirt — make it visible.
  • Step 3 — Walk to your front door and open it with your key. Pro tip: leave the key in the lock before you start recording to save time.
  • Step 4 — Go to your workspace or home office. Show your desk with a business card on it. Show your business license or any business documents. Show your computer or phone with your website open.
  • Step 5 — Submit the video immediately after recording.

Your video needs to prove three things:

  • Your location — the registered address is a real place you operate from
  • Your business exists — branded vehicle, equipment, business cards, or marketing materials
  • You are in charge — unlock a door, show business documents, display your workspace
If Your Verification Is Rejected or Stuck
If Rejected

Check your email — Google usually sends the reason. Common causes:

  • No house number or address visible at the start of the video
  • No proof of access (you didn't unlock a door)
  • Missing business documents or branded materials
  • Video too long or included talking/faces

Go back to your GBP dashboard, click "Get verified," and re-record using the checklist above. You do not need to start over.

If Stuck on Pending for More Than 5 Days
  • Do not make any changes to your profile — this can reset the clock
  • Contact GBP support through your GBP dashboard (not the general Google help form)
  • Reference your verification submission date when contacting support
