How to Get Your
Google Business Profile Verified

A step-by-step guide for home service business owners

Why This Matters

Getting your Google Business Profile verified is one of the most important steps you can take to grow your local business online. A verified profile:

  • Shows up in Google Search and Google Maps when customers look for your services
  • Signals to Google and your customers that your business is real and trustworthy
  • Unlocks reviews, messaging, posts, and booking features
  • Directly impacts how high you rank in local search results
Before You Begin — All Business Types
Step 1: Set Up Your Google Business Profile
  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in with your Google Account.
  2. Click "Add your business" and enter your business name.
  3. Select the category that best describes what you do (e.g., pressure washing service, HVAC contractor, plumber).
  4. Enter your business address or set your service area — see your business type below.
  5. Add your phone number, website, and business hours.
  6. Click "Continue" to reach the verification screen.
Step 2: Prepare Your Documents

Google may ask for supporting documents during video verification. Have these ready:

  • Business license or registration
  • Insurance certificate
  • Utility bill (with business name and address)
  • Assumed name / DBA certificate (if applicable)
  • Branded marketing materials (business cards, flyers, invoices)
Note: Make sure your business name, address, and phone number on all documents exactly match your Google Business Profile. Even small differences like "Ste" vs. "Suite" can cause a rejection.

Type 1: Service Area Businesses (SAB)

A Service Area Business goes to its customers — not the other way around. Pressure washers, plumbers, electricians, HVAC technicians, and landscapers are all SABs.

What Makes You an SAB?
  • You travel to the customer's home or business to perform work
  • Customers do NOT come to your location
  • Your business address is typically hidden on Google Maps
  • You set a "service area" instead of showing a pin on the map
How to Set Up as an SAB
  1. Enter your actual business address during setup — it will stay hidden from the public.
  2. When asked "Do you want to add a location customers can visit?", select NO.
  3. Set your service area by city names, zip codes, or counties. Stay within a realistic 2-hour driving radius.
  4. Make sure your service area on GBP matches the "areas we serve" page on your website.
Note: Never use a P.O. box, virtual office, or coworking space as your address. Google detects this and may suspend your listing. Your real address stays private — only you and Google see it.
Video Verification for SABs

One continuous, uninterrupted shot — no pausing, no cuts. Keep it under 2 minutes. Aim for 45–90 seconds. Practice the sequence once before you hit record.

SAB Video Sequence (In Order)
  • Step 1 — Start outside your home or base. Show your house number and neighboring houses so Google can confirm the address.
  • Step 2 — Show your work vehicle and the tools you use. If you have a logo on anything (vehicle wrap, decal, shirt), make it visible.
  • Step 3 — Walk up to your front door and open it using your key. Pro tip: leave the key in the lock before you start recording to save time.
  • Step 4 — Go to your home office or workspace. Show your desk with your business card on it. Show your license and business documents. Show your computer with your website or CRM open.
  • Step 5 — Submit the video immediately after recording.
SAB Pre-Flight Checklist
Before You Hit Record
  • GBP address matches your business license and utility bills exactly
  • Business website lists your service area, phone number, and business name
  • You have citations on BBB, Yelp, or your local Chamber of Commerce
  • Vehicle is branded (or extra documents and marketing materials ready if not)
  • Business documents gathered: license, registration, invoice, or utility bill
  • Business card placed on your desk
  • Key pre-inserted in your front door lock
  • Computer open showing your website, CRM, or business software
  • Domain-level email set up (e.g., you@yourbusiness.com) — not Gmail or Yahoo
Type 2: Storefront Businesses (Customers Visit You)

A storefront business has a physical location where customers come to you — like a showroom, office, shop, or service center. Your address is publicly displayed on Google Maps.

How to Set Up as a Storefront
  1. Enter your full business address. Make sure it exactly matches your business license and signage.
  2. When asked "Do you want to add a location customers can visit?", select YES.
  3. Set your business hours accurately — Google may cross-check these.
  4. Upload photos of your storefront, entrance, interior, and signage.
Video Verification for Storefront Businesses
Storefront Video Sequence (In Order)
  • Step 1 — Start outside. Show your building's street number and neighboring businesses to confirm the address.
  • Step 2 — Show your exterior signage or building directory with your business name clearly visible.
  • Step 3 — Walk to the front door and unlock it using your key.
  • Step 4 — Go inside to your workspace. Show your desk with a business card on it. Show your license and business documents.
  • Step 5 — Show your computer open to your website, CRM, or business software. Show any branded materials.
  • Step 6 — Submit the video immediately after recording.
Note: Temporary printed signage (16" x 9") is acceptable if you don't have permanent signage yet.
Type 4: Home-Based Businesses

Many service businesses — especially when just starting out — operate from home. This is completely allowed by Google, but there are specific rules to follow.

Home-Based SAB (Most Common for Contractors)

If you run your business from home and go to your customers, you are a home-based SAB. Your home address will be kept private on Google Maps.

  • Use your home address as your business address during setup — it stays hidden
  • Select NO when asked if customers visit your location
  • Set your service area by city, zip code, or county
  • Your verification video should follow the SAB Video Sequence above
  • Show your house number and neighboring houses at the start of the video
  • A home office or desk with your business card and documents is perfectly acceptable proof
If Your Verification Is Rejected

Don't panic — rejection is common, especially on the first try.

Step 1: Find Out Why
  • Check your email — Google usually sends a reason for the rejection
  • Go back to your GBP dashboard and click "Get verified" — the reason may appear there
  • Common reasons: video too long, no location proof, no proof of access, inconsistent documents
Step 2: Fix and Re-Record
  • Use the checklist for your business type and re-record your video
  • Make sure your documents show the exact same name and address as your GBP
  • Keep the video under 2 minutes with no talking or faces
  • Add more branded materials if your first video lacked them

If Verification Is Stuck on "Pending"
  • Do not make any changes to your profile — this can reset the clock
  • Contact GBP support through your GBP dashboard (not the general help form)
  • Reference your verification submission date when contacting support
After Verification — Keep Your Profile Strong
Immediately After Verification
  • Add high-quality photos: exterior, vehicle, equipment, and completed jobs
  • Write a complete business description using your main service keywords
  • Add all your services with descriptions and pricing if possible
  • Turn on messaging so customers can text you directly from Google
  • Start requesting reviews from happy customers — reviews are a major ranking factor
Ongoing Best Practices
  • Post weekly updates, offers, or job photos to your GBP to stay active
  • Respond to every review — positive and negative
  • Keep your hours, phone number, and website up to date
  • If you make a major change (address, name, phone), do ONE change at a time — this can trigger re-verification
